Matar paneer

Matar paneer is a popular North Indian dish made with green peas (matar) and Indian cottage cheese (paneer) cooked in a rich tomato-based gravy. Here's a simple recipe for making matar paneer:

Ingredients


For the gravy:
  • 2 cups ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup cashews, soaked in hot water for 15-20 minutes
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water

For the peas and paneer:

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen green peas (matar)
  • 250g paneer (cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cream (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  • by making the tomato-cashew paste. In a blender, combine the chopped tomatoes, soaked cashews, grated ginger, and minced garlic. Blend until you get a smooth paste. Set this paste aside.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  • Add the finely chopped onions and saut√© until they become translucent and light brown.
  • Add the tomato-cashew paste and cook it over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from it. This can take around 10-15 minutes.
  • Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
  • Pour in 1/2 cup of water and let the gravy simmer for another 5-7 minutes. If the gravy is too thick, you can add a bit more water to achieve your desired consistency.
  • Add the green peas (matar) and cook for about 5 minutes, or until they become tender.
  • Gently add the paneer cubes and simmer for another 2-3 minutes. Be careful not to overcook the paneer, as it can become tough.
  • If using fresh cream, add it at this stage and stir it into the gravy. This will give the dish a creamy texture.
  • Garnish the matar paneer with freshly chopped cilantro leaves.
  • Serve matar paneer hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.




Enjoy your homemade matar paneer!
