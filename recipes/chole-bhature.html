Chole bhature

Chole bhature is a food dish popular in the Northern areas of the Indian subcontinent. It is a combination of chana masala (spicy white chickpeas) and bhatura/puri, a deep-fried bread made from maida.

ingredients and steps

For Chole (Spiced Chickpea Curry):

  • Chickpeas: You can use dried or canned chickpeas. If using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight and cook until tender.
  • Onions: Chopped or sliced onions are used for the curry base.
  • Tomatoes: Tomatoes are often used to add a tangy flavor to the curry.
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: A mixture of crushed or minced ginger and garlic.
  • Cooking Oil: Usually, vegetable oil is used for saut√©ing the onions and tomatoes.
  • Spices: A combination of various spices, including cumin seeds, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, turmeric, red chili powder, and amchur (dried mango powder).
  • Salt: To taste.
  • Water: For cooking and creating the curry consistency.
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves: Chopped coriander leaves are used for garnish.

For Bhature (Deep-fried Bread):

  • All-Purpose Flour (Maida): The main ingredient for making the bhature dough.
  • Yogurt: Yogurt is used to make the dough soft and fluffy.
  • Baking Powder: It helps in making the bhature puff up when deep-fried.
  • Salt: To taste.
  • Sugar: A small amount for flavor.
  • Cooking Oil: For deep-frying the bhature.
These are the basic ingredients for chole bhature. There may be variations in the recipe, and some people like to add additional ingredients like green chilies, asafoetida (hing), and bay leaves for extra flavor. It's a flavorful and satisfying dish that is often served with pickles, onions, and sometimes a side of yogurt or raita.
